near:

Near is a file explorer system aiming to explore fundamental changes to how we browse, find and group files. We are experimenting with unique ideas to guage their usefulness and practicality in the real world when trying to organise and find the files you want.

Clasically file explorers have been a one to one presentation of the file tree of the system. We aim to break that by providing tools to allow users to 'transform' how the files are presented to them, we call these virtual transformation. Virtual transformations are modifications to how the files are shown to the user without actually modifying the hierarchy on disk. For example you can 'rebase' one file/folder to another without affecting any of the paths on the system. This lets you reorganize your data into a form that is better suited for the job at hand without affecting other processes that may rely on the existing hierarchy established on the system.

Another feature Near is exploring are what we call slices. A slice is very similar to a search but rather than showing a flat list of results it preserves the hierarchy shown to the user. You are still able to move in and out of folders but only the relevant contents are shown to the user.
